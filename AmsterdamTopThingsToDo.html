Top Attractions in Amsterdam

Amsterdam is a city where the ancient canals lined up with gabled edifices make for an alluring picture-perfect site whereas you’ll spot a large number of people at the city’s treasure-packed museums, vintage shops, or amazing design, drinking, and dining scenes. Right from the 17th century, this city has been the hub of trade activities and with two World Heritage Sites and numerous other historically and culturally prominent places coupled with a beautiful landscape, Amsterdam is one of the most visited international tourist destinations today. With rich cultural heritage attracting a lot of visitors, it was also home to luminaries like Anne Frank, Rembrandt van Rijn, Vincent van Gogh and many others. Get clicked at the crisscrossing canals which add to the fairy tale feel of the city or explore the historical, architectural and cultural aspects of the city. If you are a crazy bike tourist, wish to have a romantic boat ride, savor cheese, or want to indulge in some adult entertainment, then Amsterdam is the place for you!

Anne Frank House:

A world-renowned attraction and a popular tourist touchpoint in the country, Anne Frank House belongs to the Nazi Holocaust survivor and a Jewish girl, Anne Frank. Walk through the rooms where she and her family survived for over 2 years; though empty now, breathe the atmosphere of that time. Anne's original diary, as well as other notebooks, are on display here. You can also hop on a "virtual journey" which will help you access information about people in hiding and World War II.

Rijksmuseum:

One of the world's most renowned museums, Rijksmuseum is the Dutch National Museum and illustrates the art and history of Holland from Middle Ages to the present. Visit the Night Watch, Gallery of Honor, centuries-old doll houses, Delft Blue Pottery, Cuypersbibliotheek as well as 8,000 objects spread across 80 rooms in the museum depicting the history of 800 years of Dutch art and history.

Van Gogh Museum:

Located at the Museum Square in the Southborough, this is an art museum dedicated to the works of Vincent Van Gogh and contains the largest collection of his paintings. Attracting visitors from all corners of the globe, it houses more than 200 paintings, 500 drawings along with works of other artists that include his fellows impressionists and postimpressionists and stands among the 25 most popular museums of the world.

De Wallen:

Undoubtedly the largest and most popular red-light district in the world, De Wallen in Amsterdam consists of a web of passageways believed to contain 300 one-room cabins rented by sex-workers, peep shows, sex shops, bordellos, condom shops, sex museums and prostitutes in red-lit windows. One of the most renowned international destinations for prostitution, the region is a top tourist destination.

Keukenhof:

One of the largest flower gardens in the world, Keukenhof is also popularly known as the Garden of Europe. Located in small town of Lisse, there are over 7 million flower bulbs that are planted annually in the garden blanketing its area of 79 acres. Tip-toe through the flowers in every color of the rainbow and take some pictures while enjoying in the lap of nature.

Canal Cruise:

The canals are the highlight of Amsterdam, making it one of the most favorite tourist destinations across the world. Having more than 100 kilometers of canals with 90 islands and 1,500 bridges, the site has been enlisted in the UNESCO World Heritage List and is often referred to as the Venice of the North. The 3 main canals in the city are Herengracht, Prinsengracht, and Keizersgracht that were constructed in the Dutch Golden Age.

Vondelpark:

Spanning across an area of 47 hectares, the Vondelpark in Amsterdam welcomes 10 million visitors annually and is a major point of attraction in the city. Being a part of the borough of Amsterdam-Zuid, it is an abode to an open-air theater, playground, and HORECA facilities. Head here on a sunny day and experience a relaxed atmosphere at one of the major hangout spots for hippies.

Heineken Experience:

One of the classic destinations for beer-guzzlers, the Heineken Experience is a fantastic brewery in the center of the city and has been transformed into an astonishing interactive tour. Visited by millions from across the world, it is a high-tech hypermedia experience and includes a self-directed walk, tasting session, interesting information about the company's history, logo, and much more.

Natura Artis Magistra:

Labeled as the oldest zoo in the country and mainland Europe, Natura Artis Magistra is a beautiful zoological garden situated right in the hub of Amsterdam. Founded in 1838, it boasts of an aquarium, planetarium, arboretum, as well as a modest collection of art. An abode to 27 monumental buildings, experience the secret life of canals at the aquarium, travel through time in the planetarium, see numerous species of birds at the Bird House, and surround yourself with butterflies in the Butterfly Pavilion.

Dam Square:

When in Amsterdam, don't miss out on the Dam Square. Residing in the original location of the shores of the dam built on River Amstel, Dam Square is famed for its buildings and frequent events that make it a prominent location for the Dutch and the tourists. Following a turbulent history, today, it is a peaceful place and an abode to scores of pigeons and several street performers.
