Best Place to Stay in Corfu

Most popular one in the seven Ionian Islands, Corfu is one of the first Greek islands to suffer from the excess of the commercial tourism. The beauty of the place successfully draws a million admirers from all around the globe. The modern Corfu town follows the cosmopolitan culture while the villages have the sleepiness of a country side engraved into their lush and green, escaping the clutches of tourism altogether. But as a tourist one of the most fundamental questions you answer is your accommodation. Here is a list of the top 3 places for accommodation for a tourist in Corfu to help you make an informed decision about your liking. So give it a read before choosing your lodging:

1. Lefkimi Village

The second largest town of Corfu, located on the southern island, Lefkimi is the most traditional village of Corfu built amidst lush green surroundings along with fertile plains full of olive trees and vineyards. Giving an ideal countryside verve, this village is a postcard-perfect destination replete with narrow streets, tall houses, lively squares and beautiful churches. Nothing beats the serenity of this village for a peaceful family holiday. Some of the must visit churches in the region include Agioi Theodoroi and Agios Arsenios, renowned for their intricate architecture ringing with historical significance. Infinite lodging facilities make it easier for the people to find a place to rest for the night.

2. Mandouki

Placed between the Monastery of Panagia Platytera and the port, Mandouki is one of the most historic neighbourhoods of the islands with a majority of the architecture ranging back to the 17th and 18th century old traditional Venetian style. This is one of the best places to stay for a variety of reasons including the proximity to port and the easy accessibility to the restaurants, cafeterias and gift shops.Besides that, it is a beautiful and picturesque suburb crossed by a narrow central road and stoned and paved roads.

3. Pelekas Village

Another authentic one in the list, Pelekas is situated at a distance of just 13kms away from Corfu Town. Atop one of the most beautiful hills surrounding the island, this place is famous for its panoramic views boasting spectacular beaches and beautiful perfect views of sea. Though the area lacks fancy infrastructure, the beaches still manage to attract hordes of sun-bathers, water sports fans and swimmers. Another one advocating the stay here is the plethora of restaurants and cafes in the vicinity.

For a typical wanderlust style exploration, venture across the cypress-studded hills in the fertile interiors of this atoll, where cobalt blue waters are fringed by the sandy coves. Corfu is the best answer to travellers seeking more beyond the history of Athens and while walls of Santorini!
