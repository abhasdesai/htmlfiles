Top Attractions in Dubrovnik

Whether it is the vivid emerald waters of the Mediterranean or the walls and structures of the city that lend it an old world charm, Dubrovnik has every kind of attraction for the holidaying tourists from all over the world. The city is blessed with loads of sunshine almost all parts of the year, and the beaches offer the best of sunbathing and water sports for the ocean lovers. The remote cave and coves are hidden treasures to be explored by the adventure minded whereas the beautiful harbors themselves are a vision to behold. The old churches and the buildings in the city are not just architectural marvels; these offer a slice of the glorious past the city had witnessed in the olden days, long before tourism had opened the charms of this pretty city to the rest of the world. The sights are as enchanting during the night as they are on day time as these magnificent buildings become all the more alluring when illuminated at night. The experience becomes complete if accompanied by a classical concert or a live jazz performance at a bar. Not to forget the ‘Game of Thrones’ locations are a popular attraction for the TV show fans. Head here to soak in history and get a glimpse of the best of Croatia!

Ancient City Walls:

While in Dubrovnik, take a walk along the city's famous wall that surrounds the coastal area of the town in southern Croatia. Dating back to the 10th century, the wall belongs to the medieval period and are considered as a defensive barricade for the city. It joined the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1979 and has become a major tourist spot in the city.

Gundulić’s Square:

A former hub for all the locals who went for grocery shopping, Gundulić’s Square is now a famous tourist attraction and a place to buy authentic Dubrovnik products. You can choose from a range of products like handcrafted souvenirs, sweet-bitter arancini, lavender bags for closets, dried figs and almonds, or anything else that comes to your mind. A picture-worthy moment in this place is the flight of pigeons during noon when they are being fed; keep your cameras ready as this happens only once a day.

Dubrovnik Cable Car:

Enjoy the breath-taking panoramic views of the surrounding areas of Dubrovnik and the Adriatic Sea from the top of Srd Hill or via the cable car that starts from the Old Town area of the city called Ploce and transports you to a height of 405 meters above sea level in a matter of 5 minutes. The cable car services started in 1969 and still remain very popular among tourists.

Old Town:

This historical city is one of the world’s best-preserved trading ports with its beguiling architecture and scenic beauty, specifically that of the jagged limestone mountains. This quaint area is enclosed by a strong stone wall and has several churches, monasteries and palaces, and is also a part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site list.

Rector’s Palace:

A distinguished monument depicting Renaissance art and architecture, Rector's Palace is a Gothic palace with certain Baroque additions. The present structure is a mixture of styles blended, additions made and reconstruction carried out by a number of architects throughout its coarse history. The building houses a famous scientific library of Baltazar Bogisic that has more than 30,000 books and collection of almost 1,000 letters by prominent people.

Dubrovnik Cathedral:

A Roman Catholic cathedral in the city, Dubrovnik Cathedral also known as the Assumption Cathedral is the seat of the Diocese of the city and as well as a major landmark. 4 Corinthian columns encircle the main portal of the facade, a large Baroque window with a triangular gable covers the central part and features a grand Baroque dome at the intersection of the nave and the transepts. The cathedral also has several important paintings and a large treasury.

Dominican Monastery:

An architectural marvel, Dominican Monastery was built in 14th century in Gothic-Renaissance style and stands as a famous treasury of cultural and art heritage. The religious complex resembles a fortress due to its design and construction and it also has a remarkable art collection having priceless pieces of art some of the renowned artists. With the interiors richly decorated, the main highlight is the large golden Crucifix fixed in the central arch above the main altar.

Lapad Beach:

Lapad is a beautiful peninsula featuring some of the most scenic beaches in its vicinity. The bay has a long boulevard with many coffee bars to keep you refreshed. Sunbathe and enjoy the cool breeze combined with serene views and sound of the waves. There are many adventurous opportunities provided at this sandy shore to give you an adrenaline rush.
