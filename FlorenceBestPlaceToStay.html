Best Place to Stay in Florence

Florence, a grand, beautiful and wonderfully frescoed Renaissance city in Europa, is high on every avid traveler’s bucket list. Gorgeous piazzas graced by marble statues, infinite history of the best artists of all times, Florence lets you have a perfect time-travel experience with its many converted old family homes and medieval hotels. A focal point for art aficionados, the top 3 places for accommodation for a tourist in Florence are perfectly centered for those who wish to reach their favorite spots in the blink of an eye. So whether you are here to explore the rich cultural scene of the city or unwind the nightlife in Florence, check out the best places to stay in Florence:

1. Duomo

Florence’s bulls eye, The Duomo is for all those planning a short weekend-getaway to Florence. If you wish to be absolutely centrally located, wasting no time in walking to the main touristy spots, and don’t mind splurging on your vacay, then this is just the place to be. Bearing the entire city’s action, Duomo boasts Piazza Della Signoria as its main square. Popular attractions like the Palazzo Vecchio, the Cathedral and Uffizi Gallery will be at a stone’s throw from wherever you base yourself in this neighborhood in Florence. This district is aptly convenient to be connected to other parts of the town, but this also means it is always thriving with tourists.

2. Santa Croce

If you wish to be centrally located but away from the entire tourist bustle, try Santa Croce. This district in Florence is ideal to give you a local-like feel while keeping in sync with the medieval Florentine art and architecture. Santa Croce offers an impeccable nightlife, upscale shopping avenues and cozy cafes and restaurants, while letting you get sandwiched between the main spots of Oltrarno and Duomo. The area’s main attraction is the massive Basilica, a final resting place for many world-famous Italian artists and thinkers including Galileo and Michelangelo. Don’t miss out on the aperitivo hour to catch up with the locals and get the whole Florentine vibe.

3. Santa Maria Novella

For hardcore budget travelers who also wish to be based in one of the main area of Florence, nothing beats the eclectic neighborhood of Santa Maria Novella. The area offers two sub-divisions to choose from- the safely grim streets close to the main train station and the vibrant residential area between the Church and the Arno River. You can find everything here, from lovely hotels to the more controversial ones.

For a small Tuscan town, Florence packs an appalling punch. World-class architecture, impeccable Italiano food and a whole Renaissance history, Florence is a true underdog for Euro-travelers!
