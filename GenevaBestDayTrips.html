Best Day trips from Geneva

The city of Geneva, an important contributor to the Inventory of Swiss Heritage Sites welcomes you! Kick off your journey with a pinch of religious touch. Stop by Cathedral St-Pierre et Chapel des Macchabés or Notre Dame Church. The main archeological sites like the Foundation Baur, Museum of the arts d'Extrême-Orient and Parc et Campagne de la Grange are some rare sites hard to find anywhere else in the continent. Then come the museums, theaters and other cultural sites here; things like The Opera House, the Grand Theater de Genève, popular for hosting the largest stage in Switzerland. It features opera, dance performances, recitals, concerts and theatre. The Victoria Hall is a must visit too. With numerous museums and various art galleries in the city, this place is also a home to the United Nations headquarters which can also be visited. Geneva, thousand delights in a single city! An ideal location in the heart of Europe, Geneva offers unforgettable experiences in a one of a kind natural setting! From the shores of the biggest lake in Western Europe to its breathtaking views, Geneva never ceases to amaze time and again. This picturesque city makes you enjoy to the fullest. You will find yourself being spellbound by its diverse and enchanting landscape. Geneva has everything it takes to ensure that you have a magical experience, one that will leave you completely awestruck. But don’t you think that’s all there is in Swiss! Besides the infinite endowments of this city, here are some of the handpicked weekend break destinations for the Best Day Trips from Geneva:

1. Chillon Castle

A blend of elegance and history awaits your arrival! The walls of the 9th century Chillon Castle rising from the waters of Lake Geneva in one of the Europe's most dramatically aesthetic settings. Costumed guides add up to the experience with ancient stories that bring the castle to life. The highlights are the kitchen, banqueting hall, Bernese Room, Heraldic Hall, the Duke's apartments, St. George's Chapel and the Count's Great Hall with its splendid coffered ceiling and Gothic styles. The most preferred mode of transport among people from Geneva to Chillon Castle is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 1 hour 45 minutes. Other travelling options include hiring a taxi which will take you to the destination in nearly the same time.

Mont Saleve

On the French border south of Geneva, Mont Saleve stands tall with its breathtaking views and manages to bring bundles of joy in your day trip with never ending outdoor activities! This limestone ridge offers super amazing views of the lake and the Alps. Along with the views, you can enjoy rock climbing and hiking here. You may also simply take the 6 minute cable car from Veyrier to an elevation of 1,143 meters. Between the Petit and Grand Salève is a half an hour climb to the summit of the Petit-Salève. At 1.5 kilometers from the Monnetier, a road on the right leads to magnificent scenes of the Mont Blanc chain and Lake Geneva. At 1,308 meters, this is the highest point on the Grand Salève. Eureka! The best way to get from Geneva to Mont Saleve is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 40-45 minutes. Other travelling options include a bus which would take 50 minutes. Alternatively, you can even rent a taxi that will take you to the destination in around 40 minutes.

3. Annecy

A nearly perfect natural setting beside a mountain backed lake, it undoubtedly is hard to beat the French town of Annecy! Get to learn more about the town's history at the museum inside the Château d'Annecy, a restored castle that brightens up the old town, the Vieille Ville in grace. Stroll around its narrow streets and enjoy the scenery of medieval houses that line its canals. Have good food and fun wandering around the ever pleasing paths of nature. To get from Geneva to Annecy, the most preferred option both by locals and spendthrift tourists, is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 45-50 minutes. Alternatively, you can save some load on your pocket and book your seat in a bus taking about 1 hour and 10 minutes to get there. If you don’t prefer public transit, a taxi can also be hired which will take you to the destination in around 50 minutes.

4. Lausanne

The center of business and commerce, Lausanne is yet another beautiful city and an interesting place to visit for day tripping. The streets here are unique; climb steeply! If you are a sports person, good news for you! Lausanne is the world headquarters of the Olympics. Sports is in this place's veins. Make a visit to the remarkable museums exhibiting fine arts, art brut and photography as well as Lausanne's Roman past here if you are much of a curious traveler. The most preferred mode of transport among people from Geneva to Lausanne is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 1 hour. Other travelling options include bus services that would take 1 hour and 30 minutes to get you there. If not that, a taxi can also be hired which will take you to the destination in around 1 hour and 10 minutes.

5. Vevey

One of the prettiest towns on the lake, Vevey is a blend of medieval buildings with Belle Epoch style. It feels like the second home. At its center is one of Europe's largest market squares, filled with local farmers, food producers, florists and craftspeople. Homely! Vevey was the home of Charlie Chaplin which justifies why his statue over shines the lake. Do not forget stopping by the excellent Alimentarium, an interactive museum offering you food history, sources, traditions, lore and science. Vevey is known for both its dining and its shopping. Just do not miss out on these too! The most preferred mode of transport among people from Geneva to Vevey is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around an hour. Other travelling options include a bus which would take 1 hour and 35 minutes. Alternatively, a taxi can also be hired which will take you to the destination in around 1 hour and 15 minutes.
