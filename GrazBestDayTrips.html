Best Day trips from Graz

Graz is a city where classic architecture blends seamlessly with the modern influx. There is no shortage of historical places in Graz that paint a picture of the region from the middle ages and adding to its uniqueness, it is regarded as a UNESCO World heritage site. The old museums standing in the city are complemented well by the modern installations. Also regarded as a University town, Graz has a large young crowd making the city all the more vibrant which is also reflected in its active nightlife. Graz has something for every type of traveller, and any type of budget. Graz also has wonderful gastronomical attractions which delight visitors with the traditional Styrian cuisine as some of the best and oldest restaurants in Austria can be found in the city. If one gets tired of the city, there are plenty of spaces to explore outside the city including prehistoric caves, breathtaking gorges, mountains and woodlands.


1. Klagenfurt

Though, this one has no intentions to compete with the urban lifestyle of Vienna or Graz, Klagenfurt is an enjoyably vibrant and sunny city with a warm welcoming crowd. With an easy access to the lakeside villages on and around the Wörthersee, the city itself provides you many places to just stop and admire the life float by in front of your eyes. The western limit of the place is touched by the leafy green space of Europapark in addition to the largest bathing complex. For the most comfortable journey, opt for the train taking 2 hours of rail time. Other options include buses taking 2h 30mins or cars taking barely an hour and a half of average road time for the same distance.


2. Vienna

Having mastered in baroque streetscapes and imperial palaces, along with artistic and musical masterpieces, Vienna is one city that will draw you to its charm just by the name. Blessed with a splendid location on the banks of the beautiful Danube River, Vienna has played the gateway between the East and West Europe. The present day witnesses Vienna as a magnet to people from around the globe thirsty to witness the grandeur of sight the place has to offer. The most comfortable and time efficient journey can be completed in a car taking almost 2 hours. Other options for the commutation include train 2h 45mins and buses taking 2h 15mins.


3. Maribor

The largest and second most important city in Slovenia, Maribor is situated amidst the Pohorje Mountain, the Slovenske gorice Hills and the Kozjak Hills. Embracing with the town hills on the north and the foothills of the Pohorje Mountain on the south-eastern side, the place is an eye candy on every bucket list. Some of the sights you can't afford to miss in the vicinity include Old Vine, Vinag Wine Cellar, Lent, Slomškov Square, Castle Square and the Maribor Synagogue. Most preferred mode of transport is the train taking about an hour of travel time. Other options include buses taking the same duration as trains and cars taking cars barely 45mins.


4. Slovenia

To call the lands of Slovenia an earthly paradise would be an understatement! Featuring an array of snow-capped peaks, turquoise-green rivers and Venetian-style coastline, to find a place better than Slovenia would be a tough grind. Along with the natural blessing, the city also houses charming rustic culture, sophisticated cuisine and harmonious architecture. A simple visit to the place will explain why every other place is just not Slovenia. The most preferred journey is claimed to be through a bus taking 2h 45mins of average road time. Other options include trains taking 3h 30mins and cars taking about 2 hours.


5. Zagreb

If ever, you are quizzed about the place that is leading in every field, Zagreb is the place to answer. From culture to arts, to music and architecture, gastronomy as well as nightlife, this place has it all, earning it the title of quality capital city. The popularity of the place has ascended the ladder in the past few years making it a proper city-break destination in its own right. The most comfortable journey can be covered in a bus taking a little less than 3 hours. Other options for the travel include trains taking 4 hours and cars taking only 2 hours of travel time.
