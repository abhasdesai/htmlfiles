Best Place to Stay in Helsinki

The chilling Scandinavian beauty nestled on the southern coast of the Baltic Sea is awash with a perfect blend of tradition and contraption. Lush green parks, historic museums, striking European architecture and mouthwatering food- there is so much to unwind across the city that choosing where to stay in Helsinki can become a weak kneed. So here is a list of the top 3 areas for accommodation for a tourist in Helsinki:

1. Kauppatori

With Helsinki’s most important tourist spots located in the area surrounding Senate Square, Kauppatori turns out to be the best place to stay in Helsinki. Mostly, if you are equal parts shopaholic and wanderlust, look no further than Kauppatori! Vanha Kauppahalli- the Market Hall of this locality boasts stunning architecture in its refreshing preservation. The streets are replete with amazing eateries and cafes, offering you a perfect respite from the splurge. Visit the Presidential Palace and Helsinki Cathedral, the two closely located attractions. For people traveling with family, it’s ideally quiet with a redundant nightlife and bustle.

2. Kamppi

Located in the heart of city centre, Kamppi stations you to the centre of everything Helsinki has to offer. City’s most extravagant buildings, monuments, museums, parks and markets nestle in this upscale neighborhood. Endless buoyant hotels, restaurants, bars and clubs line the streets here, making Kamppi the best place to stay in Helsinki. Visit the legendary Esplanade Park, Natural History Museum, Tuomiokirkko (The Big White Church), The Church in the Rock and Uspenki Cathedral. Besides that, a visit to Helsinki is incomplete without a visit to one its epic public Sauna baths. Escape from the wandering at Yrjonkatu Sauna, the original one in Helsinki, before you clean up for an insane night out into one of its many bars and nightclubs.

3. Kallio

A 10 minute walk from the city centre brings you to the most populated part of Helsinki. This hipster town serves as a quintessential base for youths and party hunters. The scuttling market square of Hakaniemi feels like heaven to shoppers and epicureans. From clothing and jewelry to amazing food options- it’s a one stop shop for all. Spend some time in the Beer Park if you a fond of people-watching. Other than that, this idiosyncratic area has many noteworthy cultural spots for people who prefer London’s Hackney or Berlin’s Kreuzberg.

Helsinki is as tranquil and reserved in its cobbled streets and street musicians as sleek and modern with cutting-edge nightclubs and buildings. No wonder it’s a favored weekend getaway for Europeans and a bucket list city for the true travel bugs!
