Best Place to Stay in Lisbon

Cradled between several gorgeous hillsides right next to Rio Tejo, Portugal’s biggest city and capital Lisbon is a captivating medieval town. The postcard-perfect cobbled streets and ancient ruins of the city run parallel to its refurbished and contemporary neighborhoods. Lisbon’s hotel scene has also exploded phenomenally in the recent years, thanks to the influx of expanded tourism. So whatever your travel scene is, top 3 places for accommodation for a tourist in Lisbon offer you everything from heritage hotels to newfangled independent resorts.

1. La Baixa

The beating heart of Lisbon, Baixa is the exciting downtown area, always bustling with outdoor cafes, street artists, fancy shops and boutiques fringing the pedestrianized roads. Neo classical art is well frescoed in this riveting oblong of gigantic monuments flanked by the plazas of Rossio, Figueira and the grand Praca do Commercio riverfront. Most of the 18th century buildings have been converted into hotels and guesthouses. Neighboring Braixa is the chic district of Chiado, a perfect spot to splurge.

2. Alfama

Lisbon’s oldest neighborhood is all for romantic souls out on a honeymoon. This fascinating district of steep, winding streets sprawled like threads amidst the densely packed houses offers the best views of Lisbon. Fado restros and souvenir shops are replete in this old-world face of Lisbon. The ancient and colorful Azulejos Portuguese tiles were invented in this very area. Its placement directly below the castle makes Alfama infinitely charming and an obvious visit. If you have been to Lisbon before or are here for a weekend getaway, Alfama is the best place to stay in Lisbon.

3. Avenida Da Liberdade

Seeking a place that is both beautiful and centrally located? Try Avenida Da Liberdade. Some call it the Champs Elysees of Lisbon- designer boutiques, grand hotels and opulent eateries. The main plaza is adorned with elegant patterns of fountains and outdoor cafes. If you are an architecture aficionado, you are sure to fall for this neighborhood in Lisbon. Monument to the Heroes of the Great War is a popular attraction in this vicinity. The area is compact enough to be accessed on foot. But other than that, you can easily use public transportation to get from A to B. Popular spots like the Rossio Square, Rua Augusta Shopping Square, Sao Domingos Church and Edward VII Park are in close proximity to this area.

Cheap booze and a liberal lifestyle makes Lisbon a raucous city for travelers and night owls. Authentic Fado Houses, student dives, opulent wine bars and LGBT hotspots make it an extremely vibrant city!
