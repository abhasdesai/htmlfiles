Top Attractions in Madrid

A city like Madrid has so much to offer, that it becomes a little difficult to absorb all of it in a single go. Starting with the culture of the city, it is an understatement to say that the cultural aspects of this city are simply overwhelming. The residents of Madrid celebrate festivals with a lot of passion. Talking about the food in Madrid, it is simply a pleasure to taste the cuisines here. The delicacies here are mouth-watering and every upscale restaurant is famous for a speciality or two. Every city in Spain has a charm of its own and they are unique in one aspect or the other. But to be specific about Madrid, it has so much to offer to different kinds of travellers or tourists. The Government of Madrid has tried its level best to preserve the precious history of this country and city by constructing museums and art galleries to showcase the historical artefacts and documents. All in all, it is simply a joyride to visit this part of the country and it is sure to be an experience of a lifetime for many.

Royal Palace of Madrid:

Built in the 9th century, the Royal Palace is enriched with materials like Spanish marble and stucco, and doors made out of mahogany which are considered to be a piece of art. Tagged as one of the finest palaces in Europe, the decoration of the palace changes over time drawing inspiration from various artistic styles. Not the actual abode of the King of Spain, it is used by the Government to conduct ceremonies, official banquets, and various other functions.

El Retiro Park:

Having been beautifully designed, the Gardens of Retiro have an array of marble monuments along with landscaped lawns. Unequivocally, this is one of the most desired places in Madrid with amazing scenery that attracts a huge number of visitors. Built in 1868, there is an artificial lake (estanque) within the park and is an excellent spot to unwind.

Plaza Mayor:

Madrid's huge central square, Plaza Mayor is considered as one of the most beautiful open spaces found among the jammed streets of this beautiful city. It is undoubtedly a winning combination of imposing architecture accompanied with a set of historical tales. The square is abuzz with a vibrant street life on a daily basis and is a classic reference point in the city. Until the year of 1878, the central square used to be the place for bullfights and a lot of royal celebrations used to take place here.

Museo Nacional del Prado:

One of the most frequented places in Madrid, the Prado Museum has masterpieces from schools in Italy and other parts of Spain with around 8,600 paintings and more than 700 sculptures. Opened in 1819, it houses the most comprehensive Spanish paintings and the ambiance here will take you back to the 11th century thanks to the Mozarabic murals from the Church of San Baudelio de Berlanga. There is also an apt display of the Spanish Golden Age through the works of Ribera, Murillo and Zurbaran.

Puerta del Sol:

One of the most renowned and lively places in Madrid, Puerta del Sol is a major square with a mounted statue of King Carlos III and the spot for the radial webbing of Spanish roads that converge at the same point. You will also find the famous clock here, the bells of which indicate the traditional eating of the Twelve Grapes as well as the beginning of a New Year.

Temple of Debod:

Temple De Debod is an Egyptian temple in downtown Madrid that was saved by the Egyptian President Gamal Abdel Nasser from the rising waters of Lake Nasser. The construction of the temple that is dedicated to Amon, God of Thebes started in 2200 BC and it took a lot of time for completion. Built and decorated in a design similar to the Meroitic chapel, it is a must-visit in Madrid.

Santiago Bernabéu Stadium:

Located in the middle of Paseo de la Castellana, this stadium was originally known as the Charmatin Stadium and opened around 60 years back. Home ground of the Real Madrid team, it is deemed to be an elite stadium by the UEFA and the authority holds all kinds of events and soccer competitions here. Having a capacity of 80,000 people catapults this stadium amongst the top slots. There are guided tours which take you on a detailed journey through the nooks and corners assuring a stunning view of the stadium

Almudena Cathedral:

Madrid's only cathedral, Almudena was built in 1993 and stands majestically in front of the Royal Palace and is renowned for its sheer size. Once inside, don't forget to visit the adorned statue of Our Lady of Almudena, set right in front of the awe-inspiring altarpiece as well as the crypt known for its neo-Romanesque vaulting.

Madrid Rio:

An enormous recreational and cultural area in the heart of the city, Madrid Rio is a spacious waterside park along the Manzanares River ornamented with walking and bike trails, play areas, bridges, ponds and fountains. You may want to visit this park for the leisurely facilities it provides and the charismatic culture it houses. Have a romantic stroll with your partner, enjoy the different cultural activities or let your kid roister in one of the many play areas.

Plaza de Toros de Las Ventas:

Tagged as the most popular bullrings across the country, Plaza de Toros de Las Ventas is built in Neo-Mudéjar by the renowned architect Espeliú and should definitely be on your list while visiting Madrid. Adorned with red brick and ceramic tiles, it is divided in a ring with a diameter of 60 meters and a cluster of zones known as 'patios'. Apart from bullfights, this ring is also used for political meetings, concerts, tennis and Red Bull X-Fighters. If you are lucky enough to witness a show, do buy tickets in advance. The prices for the fights range from €5 to €147. Kindly check the website for the same.
