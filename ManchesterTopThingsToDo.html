Top Attractions in Manchester

With a great history in sports, music, and industry, Manchester is a multicultural, multifaceted city and one of the most visited British cities by international tourists. Manchester has been the hub of many rebellions and revolutions that brought about many reformations in the industrial and social structure of the country. Mainly known as a sports city for its renowned place in international football with two major clubs, Manchester also has an outstanding arts and literature spheres. A rich mix of history and culture is clearly depicted in its museums, galleries, and art centers, but what makes Manchester worth visiting is the whirl of pleasure-seeking allowing you to dine, drink and dance yourself into happy times.

Old Trafford:

Embark on a football journey at the Old Trafford which takes you behind the scenes of the world’s most renowned stadium as well as the Manchester United Museum. Labeled as the Theater of Dreams, it has a seating capacity of 75,643 and ranks 7th with respect to the area all across Europe. Home to the club of Manchester United, it is an enormous club stadium in the United Kingdom.

Etihad Stadium:

One of the best stadiums in the country, the Etihad Stadium in an enclosed building and has a bowl design with a huge seating capacity of 55,000. The most noticeable feature of the stadium are the louvers in each of the 4 corners and the roof that continuously runs around the stadium creating a spectacular effect. It is the home ground of the Manchester City Club and is well-known for its facilities.

Museum of Science and Industry:

A must-visit attraction for every age, Museum of Science and Industry fascinates its visitors through interactive exhibitions, an enormous collection of vintage vehicles, historic machinery, and much more. Explore halls filled with vast, steam-powered machines in action, learn how the Industrial Revolution started in the city, greet historical characters, watch demonstrations of original machinery, embark on a train ride behind replica steam locomotive, and enjoy the hands-on workshops. This museum is a great day out for families and children.

Manchester Museum:

You can explore the world at the Manchester Museum which was inaugurated as a natural history museum in 1821 and presently showcases works of archaeology, anthropology, and natural history. Scattered over 4 floors, the exhibits consist of minerals, animal life, rocks, aquarium, prehistoric life as well as a vivarium from across the world. Tagged as United Kingdom’s largest university museum, it acts as a major tourist attraction as well as research and teaching center.

Manchester Town Hall:

Designed in Victorian Gothic style by Alfred Waterhouse, the Manchester Town Hall was inaugurated in 1877 and is an abode to the Manchester City Council. Statues of remarkable figures from the past are housed here and it also features a glazed skylight engraved with the names of every mayor, lord mayor, and chair of the council. Don’t miss out on the mosaic pattern of bees right outside the Great Hall that symbolizes Manchester’s industry.

Manchester Art Gallery:

Encompassing a world-class art collection, the Manchester Art Gallery features magnificent 17th century to early 20th century British art along with Pre-Raphaelite canvases and artworks in a marvelous Victorian and contemporary setting. Gratify your art curiosity with an extensive treasure of 25,000 artefacts signifying both international as well as native works. Popular for its audience-focused approach, this gallery welcomes art enthusiasts from all over to enjoy its hands-on experiences and interactive programs.

National Football Museum:

Are you a football aficionado? Then look no more and visit the National Football Museum, which is renowned worldwide for having the finest collection of football artefacts and archives. Tagged as the world’s biggest and best football museum, you can eenjoy all the interactive experiences here and explore more than 2,500 objects that are scattered across 6 floors. It is one of the most visited museums in the city and maintains, safeguards and elucidates a prominent collection of football memorabilia.

Lapad Beach:

Lapad is a beautiful peninsula featuring some of the most scenic beaches in its vicinity. The bay has a long boulevard with many coffee bars to keep you refreshed. Sunbathe and enjoy the cool breeze combined with serene views and sound of the waves. There are many adventurous opportunities provided at this sandy shore to give you an adrenaline rush.
