Top Attractions in Moscow

The largest city on the European Continent, Moscow serves as the hub of country’s economy, politics, and development and this alpha global city is continuously growing as an international destination. With a great historical and cultural legacy to boast of, Moscow is the center of art, culture, and performances and is always throbbing with dance, music, and colors. Also renowned as one of the greenest capitals in the world, the city is blanketed with verdant pastures of land. Don’t forget to browse through the city’s amazing architectural delights and historic edifices which are the awe-inspiring masterpieces. Moscow has everything to pique the interest of every type of traveler, from history to modernity, from art to nightlife, also the sights and experiences that this city gives is unforgettable. With history steeped in every corner of the city, you’ll find it in the roads, squares, buildings, parks, open spaces, museums, and many other monuments that take the visitors to the eras long gone. You’ll always end up encouraging yourself to grab more of this beautiful city.

Kremlin:

Situated at the heart of the city, Kremlin is a historic encircled complex overlooking the Moskva River, Alexander Garden, St. Basil’s Cathedral, and Red Square. One of the best kremlins, it is an abode to 4 palaces, 4 cathedrals, and the surrounding Kremlin Wall with the adorned towers being the official residence of the Russian President. Marvel at its architectural and historic beauty while soaking in some political importance of the palace.

Red Square:

The largest and most famed square in Moscow, the Red Square acts as a magnet for all locals as well as visitors to the city. Encircled by prominent edifices all the major streets radiate from this point and is thronged by people all day long. Dating back to the 15th century, it is renowned as a famous public marketplace as well as meeting point for centuries. The major highlights comprise of the 16th-century Baroque Basil’s Cathedral, the State Historical Museum and the massive GUM Department Store. The square hosts performances, concerts and varied themed events.

St. Basil’s Cathedral:

Locally known as St. Basil’s or Cathedral of Vasily the Blessed, this spiritual abode is Moscow’s most renowned and photographed sites. Famed for its great design and intricate architecture, it is a beautiful blend of colors, patterns, and shapes unique to Russian style. It is positioned majestically near the Kremlin and has witnessed various historical and political events of the country.

Gorky Park:

Laid out in 1928, Gorky Park stands as the central park of rest and culture and is one of the most prominent spaces of Moscow. Stretching along the banks of Moscow river, it has been divided into 2 parts with one being jammed with attractions and rides of interest to children whereas the other area comprises of gardens and woodland. Entertain yourself and children at a diverse range of rides and activities ranging from roller coasters to bungee jumping or get clicked at numerous old building dating back to 18th and 19th centuries. Other activities include bicycling, rollerblading, table tennis, volleyball, urban and extreme sports as well as pétanque.

Bolshoi Theater:

Standing as Moscow’s symbolic site, Bolshoi Theater represents Russian classical architecture and renowned as the 2nd biggest opera house across Europe. With a charming and romantic atmosphere, you may head here to gaze at the immortal creations and admire the elegant edifice. Coupled with state-of-the-art sound as well as lighting facilities and an intimate atmosphere, the genuine lovers of ballet and opera can research a bit before heading for any performance whereas the rest of the visitors can take in the breathtaking beauty of the setting. There are various shows performed here which range between 100 RUB and 15000 RUB.

Gostinyy Dvor:

One of the most beautiful architectural marvels of the city, Gostiny Dvor is a massive center of business, commerce, and leisure. Encircled by stucco columns and classic style arches, you’ll spot restaurants, cafes, banks, offices, beauty salons, and shops inside this edifice. If you wish to fill your bags with Russian products, this is a good place to head. This attraction is also renowned as a prestigious exhibition space across the country.

The Pushkin State Museum of Fine Arts:

Split over 3 branches and boasting of a broad selection of European works, Pushkin Fine Arts Museum stands as a massive European art museum in Russia. Art aficionados can find paintings, sculptures, and applied art from historic times to present date whereas the major treasures are unraveled in European art collection from 1600, small collection from old Egypt, and rooms filled with plaster casts of prominent sculptures from the Hellenistic era until the Renaissance.

Memorial Museum of Cosmonautics:

Unravel the country’s 20th-century space travel attainments at Memorial Museum of Cosmonautics that specialize in space exploration. Standing as the only one of its kind, take a look at around 85,000 exhibits depicting space technology, astronomy and space travel along with taxidermy space dogs, Belka and Strelka. Dive into history with the exhibits, try your hand at the fun gadgetry, and gaze at the excellently conceived display depicting how astronauts survive a space flight.

Lenin’s Mausoleum:

Also renowned as the Lenin’s Tomb, this wooden mausoleum was perched on the square and layered in red, black, and grey granite that blend really well. Dating back to 1924, this stands as a major tourist attraction exhibiting some of the most interesting elements such as the Step Pyramid, Temple of the Inscriptions, the Tomb of the Cyrus the Great, and the most renowned “modern mummy” across the world. The major highlight is that it is the resting place of the leader of the Bolshevik Revolution Vladimir Lenin.

Moscow Zoo:

Spanning an area of 20 hectares, Moscow Zoo stands as the largest zoo across Russia and exhibits an exceptional horde of northern animals as well as many exotic species. You can spot over 6000 animals at this fauna abode belonging to over 1000 species. Founded in 1864, the zoo is also renowned to study reproduction, breeding, and the breeds of the rare endangered species. Head here with your family and let your children gain some knowledge.
