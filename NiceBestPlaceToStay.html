Best Place to Stay in Nice

People who know nothing beyond Paris and Cannes in France need to take a look at Nice. A melting pot of varied cultures and economic classes, Nice poses the best place for holiday for those looking for an ideal beach vacation, but with a tinge of history and medieval architecture. So if you are convinced to plan a sunny city break to this gorgeous French island, check out these top 3 places for accommodation for a tourist in Nice:

1. Place Massena

This is the primary, bustling hub for commercial activity in Nice. Located in the Old town at the edge of Avenue Jean Medecin, Place Massena is ideal for first time visitors to Nice with several main boulevards crossing roads here. Forget taxis and buses and explore the heart of Nice on foot or in one of their authentic trams. It’s a shoppers’ paradise and a mecca for night owls. For historians too, the scorched red walls and blue shutters are an excellent site to photograph. But the location of this district makes it way too opulent and expensive for travelers.

2. Vieille Ville

This is one of the most vibrant neighborhoods of Cote d’Azur located at the base of Castle Hill. The place has a lot of Italian influence since Nice was a part of the Savoy Kingdom earlier. The narrow, winding streets of this quaint district are fringed by magical, pastel colored Italian architecture. Visit Place Rossetti, the central square of this neighborhood bustling with cafes, gelato and cathedrals. Then there is Cours Saleya open air market for avid shoppers and food and flower lovers. Try Salad Niscois, Nice’s local cuisine revered by gourmands. This area offers reasonably priced accommodations, hence a top pick for budget travelers.

3. Promenade de Anglais

This long seaside promenade stretching along the Mediterranean coast offers panoramic vistas to wake up to from your hotel room. If you are done with the touristy sites of Nice and wish to extend your leisure vacation, then this district offers a perfect beach access to unwind and booze. Sunbathers and happy locals flock to this area in the daytime, while nights are loud and noisy with the abundant nightlife here. Many beachfront hotel fringe the walkway, ready to leave a hole in your pockets.

Nice is insanely popular among European weekend vacationers, but still unexplored by the rest of the world. This is what makes it all the more beautiful!
