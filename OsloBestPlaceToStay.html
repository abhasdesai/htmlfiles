Best Place to Stay in Oslo

Oslo, the open-air Scandinavian capital is a compact city boasting gorgeous beaches and parks. The typically European architecture and classical vistas exhibit some of the finest museums, opera and fish cuisines, not to mention the epic Northern Lights that entice thousands of visitors every winter. But finding an accommodation in this big-ticket town can cost you an arm and a leg. So follow our Guide on where to stay in Oslo, a listicle catering to all lifestyles and budgets.

1. Karl Johans Gate

Looking for luxury? Karl Johans awaits you with Oslo’s most premier hotels and top-class tourist attractions. Whether you wish to mingle outside the National Theatre, be in close proximity to the first-rate rooftop restros or wake up to Oslo’s Cathedrals in the morning, downtown area is the place to be! City centre is all about action and commotion. Travelers who seek to live a city instead of just touring across should choose this option for accommodation. If you go a little South of Karl Johans Gate, you will find yourself in the legendary Old Town, a neighborhood near the harbor boasting the Oslo’s medieval fortress- Akershus Festning.

2. Grunerlokka

Seeking something unique for a stay, try Grunerlokka! This quaint neighborhood north of Oslo’s main station has a multicultural vibe and offbeat ethnicity. Just 10 minutes away from downtown area, the ‘coolest locality’ of Oslo is a great alternative to the wallet-hungry city centre. Earlier known for its gray, industrial tapestry, Grunerlokka now hosts the most eclectic bars, boutiques, street art and art galleries of Oslo. Popular as Oslo’s Hipster Paradise, it’s a perfect haven for visitors seeking an alternate vibe of Oslo.

3. Frogner

The western end of the city is leafier and classier, mostly a pick for tourists who seek both comfort and easy access. Right behind The Royal Palace is Frogner, an area with a lot of guesthouses and B&Bs that offer you a fantastic view of the Vigeland Sculpture Park. This most popular attraction of Oslo is an ideal picnic spot of the city. Besides, for epicureans seeking best restaurants in Oslo, shopaholics looking for some splurge in upscale boutiques or art-lovers exploring cutting-edge galleries, the locality of Frogner fits the bill!

Oslo is high up in the world’s most expensive cities’ list. But you can always fetch a good deal when the question of where to stay in Oslo arises. Just make sure you make the booking well in advance to get the maximum bang for your buck!
