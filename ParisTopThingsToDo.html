Top Attractions in Paris

Undoubtedly one of the most beautiful and romantic cities on earth, Paris is world famous for its architecture and boasts of some beautiful, large avenues, Haussmannian buildings, quaint neighborhoods, lively historic squares, narrow cobblestoned streets and magnificent mansions from the 17th century. The city is also a hotspot for food and fashion enthusiasts with 70 Michelin-starred restaurants and innumerable boutiques. The city also attracts a fair share of art lovers, thanks to the finest collection of Impressionist paintings anywhere in the world housed in art galleries and museums dedicated to art. An abode to several UNESCO sites and romantic spots, head here with your loved one and have the best of your lifetime. With all the goodness that Paris has to offer, it's not really a question of 'why visit Paris", it's rather 'Why not?'

Eiffel Tower:

An iconic landmark of Paris and a world-renowned site, Eiffel Tower is a sight to behold while in you are in the city, especially when it is grandly illuminated at night. Named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, it is a beautiful wrought-iron tower built in 1889 and is the tallest structure in Paris. This architectural and romantic marvel is now a famous symbol of French civic pride and makes for an excellent photo location flocked by millions of tourists every year. You will be able to spot this exemplary structure from different places in the city. The top floor of the Eiffel Tower is known to be one of the most romantic spots in the world is an ideal setting for your amorous gestures.

Cathédrale Notre-Dame de Paris:

Located on the Île de la Cité, the Cathedral Notre Dame de Paris is a beautiful, medieval Catholic Church in Paris where Napoleon Bonaparte was crowned the Emperor in December 1804. The majestic cathedral is among the exceptional examples of French Gothic architecture and draws tourists from all over the world, regardless of their religion. Among the most notable features of the cathedral are the west front, the Galerie des Chimères, the King's Gallery as well as the 3 west portals and a rooftop that offers the most magical backdrop of the city.

Champs-Élysées:

The Champs-Élysées is one of the best avenues in the world and is home to the city's most popular restaurants, shops, boutiques and historic monuments. The boulevard runs between the Place de la Concorde and the Place Charles de Gaulle and is known for its monuments and symmetrical landscaping. It also hosts key annual events such as the Bastille Day military parade, the arrival of the Tour de la France and the Christmas lights.

Luxembourg Palace:

Lying on the border between Saint-Germain-des-Prés and the Latin Quarter, Luxembourg Palace is the seat of French Senate and was declared a National Palace in 1791. Built in 1625 by Salomon de Brosse for Queen Marie of Médicis, it is a fine example of French classical architecture dating back to the 17th century. Spend a relaxing weekend afternoon at the gardens encircling the palace as they exemplify an amazing work of landscaping.

Disneyland:

Located in the Paris suburb of Marne-la-Vallée, Disneyland Paris is a popular theme park complex featuring family-friendly rides, shows, and costumed characters. Strike a pose with your favorite Disney characters or dive into a treasure trove of thrilling activities and entertainment options that are sure to enthrall you. Spanning across an area of 4,800 acres, it is an abode to 2 theme parks in addition to a shopping, dining, and amusement complex. The most-frequented theme park across the whole of Europe, this wonderland has something special for all age groups.

Arc de Triomphe:

Standing tall in the middle of Place Charles de Gaulle, Arc de Triomphe is an iconic triumphal arch and falls among the prominent memorials in Paris providing a glimpse of French history. It was commissioned by Napoleon in 1806 to commemorate his victories and recognizes those who fought and died for France in the French Revolutionary wars and Napoleonic wars. You can appreciate the intricate design and engraving as well as enjoy spectacular landscape of the city from the terrace.

Louvre Museum:

If you are visiting Paris, you ought to experience the magic of the iconic Louvre. A central landmark of Paris, the Louvre Museum is known as the largest museum across world built over a span of 4 centuries that houses thousands of classic and modern masterpieces ranging from Roman sculptures to Da Vinci's Mona Lisa. With its ornate ceilings and walls, the Louvre adds so much dynamism and charm to Paris.

Pantheon:

Pantheon is an 18th-century mausoleum with colonnaded facade located in the Latin Quarter in Paris. Originally an abbey dedicated to St. Genevieve, it houses the remains of notable French citizens. Underneath there are catacombs, where many of France's most notable people are buried. Visitors can enjoy great panoramic views of the city from its colonnade.

Parc de la Villete:

Stretching over an area of 55.5 hectares, Parc de la Villette is the third largest as well as the most frequented park in Paris. Lying on the north-eastern edge of the city, this urban cultural park has great cultural spaces, a theatre, and activity areas for children and adults. Famed worldwide for its beautiful architecture and urban organization, there is also a water canal passing through the park adding to the awe-inspiring scenery.

Le Centre Pompidou:

Centre Georges Pompidou is an architecturally avant-garde complex of buildings that houses the National Museum of Modern Art, library and music center. The works of famous artists have been displayed chronologically over 2 sections and in addition to its permanent reserves, internationally celebrated exhibitions are conducted here every year on the top floor. The building itself is an architectural masterpiece and offers some of the best views of Paris from the top.
