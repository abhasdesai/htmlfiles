Top Attractions in Reykjavik

Reykjavik is a place of exciting possibilities encircled by stunning landscapes where countless adventures entice and numerous natural wonder await. The world’s most northerly capital attracts visitors with its natural beauty jam-packed with glaciers, waterfalls, lava fields, rainbows, streams, and mountain ranges. The Blue Lagoon is a large expanse of naturally heated pastel-blue water set amidst a jet black lavas cape and attracts people for the entire world. Along with the artistic history of the European culture, the town also offers varied range of adventure sports ranging from mountain biking to river rafting. Many museums and turf houses are located in Reykjavik that highlight the history of fishermen, trade and countrymen. This unique little island has a lot to offer along with its natural wonders and combines kaleidoscopic edifices, quirky, creative people, crazy nightlife, stunning design, and a volatile soul.

Hallgrimskirkja:

A Lutheran Parish church in the city, Hallgrimskirkja stands as a major spectacle, thanks to its stunning tower that can be viewed from anywhere in the city. The major highlight is the gargantuan pipe organ that rises to a height of 15 meters and is driven by 4 manuals, 102 ranks, 72 stops and 5,275 pipes that are designed to reproduce powerful notes. A fine example of quintessential northern European church with beautiful architectural lines, the view that it offers from the top is breathtaking.

Imagine Peace Tower:

A brilliant work of art devised by Yoko Ono, the Imagine Peace Tower exudes wisdom, healing, and joy and spreads awareness that peace and love connect all lives on earth. It comprises of numerous individual lights that unite together to form a single beam out of which 6 lights travel through the corridor encircling the well and are reflected upwards to the sky with mirrors. The words 'Imagine Peace' have been engraved on the well in 24 different languages.

Whales of Iceland:

Inaugurated in the heart of the city's old harbor area, visit Whales of Iceland to dive into the world of whales. Largest of its kind in the world, you will spot 23 life-size models of the species found in the Icelandic waters varying from the smallest marine mammals to the enormous blue whale. Browse and learn about each model supported with audio in ambient lighting giving you a feel of being underwater with them.

Perlan:

A beautiful dome-shaped structure, Perlan stands as one of the Reykjavik's greatest buildings and is an abode to the viewing platform that proffers breathtaking landscapes of the city and the surroundings. Supported by 6 gigantic hot-water tanks, it rises to a height of 26 meters and was designed by Ingimundur Sveinsson standing out from others as a sparkling jewel. Don't miss out on the revolving restaurant at the top floor.

Saga Museum:

Saga Museum is renowned for reviving memories of Icelandic history and giving an insight into the Icelandic way of life for over thousand years. Soak in the history in an educational and interactive way as you admire the lifelike replicas of historical Icelandic figures. You can opt for an audio guide that will help you immerse in the experience and gain a better perspective.

Northern Lights:

One of nature's most mysterious and beautiful phenomena, Northern Lights can be witnessed while visiting Reykjavik. Caused by an interaction of particles from the sun with the upper atmosphere, you can enjoy this natural marvel from September to April. Renowned as Aurora Borealis, spot these lights on crisp clear winter nights either whirling in shades of milky green or sporadically dazzling in a multicolored dance across the silent sky.

Golden Circle

With dreamlike scenery and an out-of-this-world feel, the Golden Circle trip is something you should surely embark upon while visiting Iceland. This 300-kilometer road is an abode to a Viking heritage site, stunning geysers, lagoons, waterfalls, and more. You will also come across the historical and geological wonder, Thingvellir National Park which allows you to visit the Geysir geothermal area.

Sólfarið - Sun Voyager:

Facing Reykjavik's alluring waterfront, Sun Voyager is a renowned steel model sculpted by Jon Gunnar Arnason. The voyager is said to resemble a Viking ship but is actually a dreamboat that pays its respects the sun. Get yourself clicked at this spot which adds to the charm of the area.

Reykjavik Botanical Garden:

Founded in 1961, the Reykjavik Botanical Garden comprises of an outdoor collection of living plants and is an abode to over 5,000 species in 8 plant types. The collection helps you gain an insight of the massive diversity of vegetation in the northern temperate zone. Head to this renowned recreational area which is one of the hidden treasures of the city.
