Top Attractions in Rome

With a huge platter of culture to offer, Rome been the center of attention and great influence since ancient times making it one of the best choices for vacation. It is definitely one of the best places for a splendid vacation filled with therelic, a dose of history and amazing food. For over thousands of years, this city has been a religious as well as a secular capital and encompasses the various developments and styles of architecture, art, and urban design, extending to a millennium of history curled up in its talon. The World Heritage sites of Rome includes a huge number of monuments such as the Mausoleum of Augustus, the Pantheon, the Mausoleum of Hadrian, the Column of Marcus Aurelius, and the Trajan’s Column. The complexity and the stratification are so much that it includes an extraordinary number of archaeological places integrated within the urban fabric, making it highly ensemble.

Colosseum:

A World Heritage Site right in the center of Rome, Colosseum is the most famed and visited amphitheater across the world spanning a construction period of over 100 years. It was the former ground of the gladiatorial games coupled with many public spectacle executions, dramas, animal hunts, and other forms of entertainment. If you happen to be in the city on Good Friday, don't miss out on the "Way of Cross" Procession that takes place around the premises every year led by the Pope and witnessed by a huge crowd every year.

Trevi Fountain

No trip to Rome is complete without paying a visit to the Trevi Fountain. As rightly said, Rome wasn't built in a day, Nicola Salvi designed this world renowned fountain and stands as a perfect example for the quote. An aqueduct 86 feet high and 160 feet wide gigantic attraction featuring Baroque architecture of Italy and without any doubt, the largest fountain in the whole wide world was built with the help of many artisans, sculptors, and craftsmen. Nestled in the historic center of the city, it is a belief that if you toss a coin in the fountain and make a wish, you'll come back to Rome. So go ahead and toss a coin and get yourself clicked at the marvelous attraction.

St. Peter’s Basilica:

A must-see site for any first time visitor to Rome, St. Peter's Basilica is the center of the entire Catholic world. Known as Italy's largest, richest and most beautiful basilica, marvel at its awe-inspiring size and architecture and you are surely going back with numerous photographs from here. Located in the papal enclave of Rome, it is also the residence of the Pope. Note: The entrance to the Basilica is free. However, the price for climbing to the top of the Dome ranges between €6 to €8.

Piazza Navona:

An architectural marvel in the heart of this romantic city, Piazza Navona stands as the most famed square of Rome dating back to the 1st century A.D. Clustered with perfect masterpieces in harmony with each other, you'll also spot street artists, numerous bars, and a classical fountain here. Head to this renowned meeting place for Romans and take back some beautiful photographs.

Castel Sant'Angelo:

Famed as Roman Emperor Hadrian's Tomb, Castel Sant'Angelo is a fortress perched on the banks of river Tiber and makes for a must-visit attraction in the city. Scattered over 5 floors, you can reach upstairs by a spiral ramp browsing through the chamber of ashes, cells where numerous historical figures were incarcerated, rooms that functioned as a Papal residence adorned with frescoes from the Renaissance period, and a large terrace serving you jaw-dropping views of the city. This fortress is a cylindrical building and houses paintings and furniture collections of the Renaissance apartments.

Spanish Steps:

Connecting the Piazza di Spagna at the base with Piazza Trinita Dei Monti at the top, the Spanish Steps are at the eastern end of the old city center. Standing as a monumental stairway consisting of 135 steps, they have butterfly shaped uneven patterns combining a blend of curves, straight flights, vistas, and terraces. At the bottom, you'll find Fontana della Barcaccia, which invites visitors to be photographed against with the Spanish Steps as a backdrop.

Baths of Caracalla:

A vast Roman thermal bath, Baths of Caracalla are vast and rectangular in shape and in ruined state now having been used from 216 A.D. to 537 A.D. Standing as a major attraction in Rome, they are designed in such a way that is not just bathing or sports spot but also used for walking and studying. On the central axis, along with excellent symmetry, one can also observe Calidarium, tepidarium, frigidarium and natation. The other striking features of this bath include the marble floorings, walls and the vitreous paste mosaics one should just not miss to notice when here.

Villa Borghese:

Known as the largest verdant shade card of green in Rome, Villa Borghese is an amazing getaway from the hustle and bustle. Sprawled over an area of 80 hectares, it stands as the 3rd largest public park in the city and comprises of buildings, museums as well as attractions in the naturalistic English manner. Stroll around the park, enjoy the nature, sip on a coffee or let your children just roister around in the park area.

Altare della Patria:

The Altare della Patria, also known as the Altar of the Fatherland, is a striking edifice situated in the Piazza Venezia all carved in white and comprised of a distinguished Corinthian colonnade and countless stairs. One of the major highlights of the building is the terrace which offers you unmatchable views of the city. Marvel at this stunning piece of architecture which rises to a height of 70 meters and dominates the area.

Roman Forum:

A majestic ramble of ruins, Roman Forum was a former showpiece center and the tall standing columns, triumphal arches, as well as remains of important edifices, still please the 21st-century visitors. The surviving remains give a glimpse of the grandeur and splendor of the Forum in ancient times. Soak in some history and let your camera do some activity for you to get numerous double-taps on your Instagram.
