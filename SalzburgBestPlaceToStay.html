Best Place to Stay in Salzburg

Those who know music know Salzburg. Recitals drift out of the main-street churches and classical music pours out of every downside street window in Salzburg. This extremely picturesque Austrian town is right out of a fairytale; a massive fortress nestled in lush green mountains on all sides, with cobblestone alleyways, brightly colored spires and a splendid river flowing through. So if you are planning a vacay to this magnificent European city, base yourself in one of t he top 3 places for accommodation for a tourist in Salzburg:


1. Mulln

Northern face of Altstadt is what they call Mulln (Mill), a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is the smallest sub-section of Altstadt boasting a country-side feel to tourists. Mullner Stadtpfarrkirche is the most popular touristy spots in this neighborhood. Mullner Brau is a popular Beer garden, the biggest in Austria, drawing thousands of beer lovers every year. Adventurers love to base themselves here in Mulln, thanks to the Monchsberg mountain right behind the church of the monastery. Hike up to the top to get some spectacular vistas of Salzburg.


2. Neustadt

This one stands tall on the site that was previously city walls and bastions. The term means New City, an alternate construction to Altstadt, The Old Town of Salzburg. Running parallel to the Linzergasse Lane, Naustadt houses many historical monuments along the Salzach River. It is merely a 10 minute walk to the city center, where all the main tourist attractions hail. It is a most preferred alternative to the old town, for there are fewer tourists and most serenity.
