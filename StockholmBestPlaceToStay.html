Best Place to Stay in Stockholm

The swanky, civilized capital of Sweden is all about history, shopping, nightlife and food! This sparkling archipelago warm with gorgeous shades of yellow and orange is circumscribed by thousands of islands, offering you ceaseless scenic beauty. With so much to see, travelers often get confused as to where to base themselves. So check out our simple guide that will help you to find the best neighborhood to stay in Stockholm.

1. Norrmalm

The heart of Stockholm, Norrmalm is also the transportation hub of the city for its direct connectivity to the Arlanda Airport. Just a few steps away from the centre is the National Museum, replete with rich Nordic paintings and Scandinavian design. Kungstradgarden houses bands playing in the summers and hardy skating in the winters, and leads you to the Royal Opera. This diverse neighborhood has a lot of designer boutiques, eateries and bars stationed in close proximity to Sturegallerian, an upscale mall. You can take a boat from Nybroplan or splurge out at The Grand Hotel in Blaiseholmen!

2. Ostermalm

This opulent locality lies to the east of Norrmalm boasting a beautiful landscape with boulevards stretching from Valhallavagen in the north to Strandvagen in the south. It is the wealthiest neighborhoods of Stockholm housing cultural blockbusters like Hallwylska Museum and the adorned Art Nouveau Kunglika Dramatiska Teatern. For gourmands, there is Ostermalmshallen, an indoor food market selling Swedish delicacies and scrumptious seafood. The rural district of Djurgarden is only a few steps away from here, while the areas of Carl Malmsten and Svenskt Tenn offer a royal Swedish touch to the area.

3. Gamla Stan

The narrow, labyrinthine cobbled streets and old spires of Gamla Stan create aa postcard-perfect aura of the city, Stockholm’s historic heart is ideal for history lovers who are in town to lose themselves to medieval alleyways like the Marten Trotzigs Grand. If you wish to live in close proximity to Kungliga Slottet- royal family’s official palace, don’t think beyond Gamla Stan. Waterfront area is fringed with restaurants and jazz clubs, making the whole area magical! Don’t miss out the grand palaces and houses on the tiny atoll of Riddarholmen, a place so perfectly preserved it looks like a movie set!

So whether you wish to party till wee hours or sightsee till sunset, take your pick from these most suitable localities in Stockholm! The city and areas around are abundant with short trips every day. So plan enough time to make the most of Sweden!
