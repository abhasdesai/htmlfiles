Best Day trips from Tallinn

If all you are searching for is a top tourist destination in Estonia with a wonderful and unique architecture, friendly people and lively bars, Tallinn does that for you! From spectacular places to stop by in plenty to having a taste of lovely cuisines, this place is bliss. Start your journey from the Town Hall Square, the heart of the Old Town. Climb the Town Hall’s tower to have a complete view of the Old Town. If heights don't scare you much, discover the wonders of the Old Town. Climb up the St. Olav’s Church, once the tallest building in the world! Catch the magnificent views and if you are a selfie freak, then you simply can’t afford to miss Kohtuotsa, one of the most photographed places in Tallinn. Do not forget Tallinn’s Town Wall. Stroll along St. Catherine’s Passage and make a visit to the Estonian History Museum’s Great Guild Hall. When it comes to museums, do not miss out on the Seaplane Harbour. If you want to combine good food with adventures, historical flavor, adrenaline rush and natural beauty, visit this place my friend. Learn more about the yum street food here. Take your family and friends along and wear your tripping pants. You need to leave back all your worries at your home and shake hands with Tallinn with a bright smile on your face. People here are perceived to be welcoming here and are sure to give a lovely time here. But hey, if someone tells you Tallinn is all there is in Estonia, don’t believe them! Here are some of the handpicked weekend break destinations for the best Day Trips from Tallinn:

1. Lahemaa National Park

Beauty personifies at Lahemaa National Park! One of the most beautiful national parks in Estonia, Lahemaa National Park is a destination you’d never want to get over with! Get to see unique species of animals including brown bears, moose and lynxes. With immensely diverse flora and fauna here, wander through magical forests and stroll around sandy beaches. Ideal for a day trip, Lahemaa National Park shines through its outstanding culture and scenery. The most preferred mode of transport among people from Tallinn to Lahemaa National Park is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 35-40 minutes. Other travelling options include hiring a taxi which will take you to the destination in around 40 minutes.

2. Haapsalu

You are SO going to fall in love with this town! With the highlights here being the unique culture of Estonia and the most beautiful white sand beaches, Haapsalu is a must visit destination. If you are into mechanics and technology, mark a visit to the Estonian train museum. Haapsalu is all about pretty culture, picturesque sites and real good selfie spots. The most preferred mode of transport among people from Tallinn to Haapsalu is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 1 hour 20 minutes. Other travelling options include a bus which would take 1 hour and 45 minutes. In case public transit doesn’t interest you, a taxi can also be hired which will take you to the destination in around 1 hour and 30 minutes.

3. Helsinki

Helsinki, the capital of Finland, welcomes you with open arms. It is said that you can seek what you may love, here in Helsinki! Visit historic museums, hit up to local bars, stroll around stunning streets and experience Finnish saunas. For those travelling for a day trip from Tallinn to Helsinki, the best mode of transport is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 3 hours. Other travelling options include hiring a taxi which will take you to the destination in around 3 hours and 10 minutes.

4. Tartu

Planning a day visit during Autumn? You need to mark Tartu as a must visit! The capital of students in Estonia, Tartu comes to life when you get to visit the oldest university in Estonia. Dare to learn more about the country’s dramatic past in the KGB museum. Spend some quality time and bring your kids to see some old toys in Tartu toy museum. You can also stop by plenty of stunning restaurants and bars, and maybe try out some delicious and traditional Estonian food. The most preferred mode of transport among people from Tallinn to Tartu is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 2 hours and 10 minutes. Other travelling options include a bus which would take 2 hours and 35 minutes. With non-availability of bus, a taxi can also be hired which will take you to the destination in around 2 hours and 15 minutes.

5. Parnu

Want to step up for a romantic date? Parnu is definitely the place to go. The beaches here are stunning and you can get to experience numerous activities you can choose from which include playing mini golf and windsurfing. When food factor pops in, it is extremely delicious in Parnu and you will be amazed to know that some locals even come here just for the pizza, arguably the best in Estonia! The most preferred mode of transport among people from Tallinn to Parnu is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 1 hour 35 minutes. Other travelling options include a bus that would take a little less than 2 hours. In case these two options don’t suit you, a taxi can also be hired which will take you to the destination in around 1 hour and 40-45 minutes.

6. Soomaa National Park

When it is about wonderful infrastructure, stroller accessible trails and catering to year long vacation moods, here is when Soomaa National Park comes into picture. During the rainy season, the park floods to the point that it can only be approached via canoes and boats, adding even more color to the whole day trip experience. If you want to see beavers and their dams, you can hike the special Beaver Trail, YAY! The best mode of transport for travelers going from Tallinn to Soomaa National Park is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 1 hour and 50 minutes. Other travelling options include hiring a taxi which will take you to the destination in more or less the same time.
