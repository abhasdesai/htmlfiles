Best Places to Party in Venice

Venice may not be highly recommended for its nightlife, but it is possible to indulge in late night entertainment with a classy meal and some crisp wine. The city has a strong tradition for opera and classical music with many churches and theaters hosting fine music concerts and shows. You can locate a few dance clubs here and there, but their functioning is limited, thanks to the stringent night law in Venice. Whatever fits your criteria of partying in the Italian town; these top 3 places to party in Venice are sure to keep you entertained. Be it cinema, concerts or dance clubs, you will find something of your taste here:

1. Dorsoduro

For Dorsoduro, the status of incorporating maximum academic institutions goes parallel with having the craziest nightlife in Venice. A heavy number of bars and nightclubs dominate this district to cater to the intellectual population here, mainly confining to the areas of Zattere, Accademia and Campo Santa Margherita. Café Rosso and Orange are most popular spots in Il Campo, which packs to capacity right from evening until late hours. Piccolo Mondo, a prime dancing venue since late 1960’s has its base near the Accademia gallery. Venice Jazz Club is another very popular spot that has its own resident band playing it all from Bossa Nova to Latin Jazz.

2. San Marco

The historic square of Piazza San Marco is the beating heart of Venice, cutting in on everything from art and culture to commerce and shopping. After dark, the crème de la Crème of Venice are spotted doing a fashion parade in and out of the many swanky restaurants and lounges here. Teatro La Fenice, a refined entertainment venue is looked up to by all theatre lovers. Cinema Rossini is another popular spot for movie buffs, host to the Venice Film Festival and many blockbuster movies. Quadri and Florian are very famous cafes, can easily be spotted with palm orchestras tinkling away in front of their porch. Aurora, Central Restaurant Lounge and the legendary Harry’s Bar are other spots worth exploring.

3. Cannaregio

Not exactly a bacchanal’s choice, Cannaregio is more laid back and unhurried compared to Dorsoduro and San Marco. With a few cool spots worth venturing into, Cannaregio hosts a lot of alternative, artistic and bohemian crowd that sings and plays music along Fondamenta della Misericordia, a perfect canal-side retreat. Il Paradiso Perduto and The Irish Pubs are two genuine bars pulling in hordes of regulars.

Venice is more of an ‘explore in daylight’ kind of town. There is so much to unwind in the day that you demand nothing more than a peaceful siesta after dusk!
