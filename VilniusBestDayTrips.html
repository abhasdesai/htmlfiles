Best Day trips from Vilnius

Vilnius, one of the most picturesque destinations where you can find the best street art, the best bars and the most epic scenic beauty is the definition of a perfect weekend break! Dive deep in the history of The Old Town of Vilnius and discover The Vilnius Castle Complex where you can find all possible defensive, cultural, and religious buildings including, Gediminas Tower of the Upper Castle, Cathedral Square and the Palace of the Grand Dukes of Lithuania. Lithuania's largest art collection is housed by the Lithuanian Art Museum! Discover the largest venue for contemporary art: The Contemporary Art Centre. Vingis Park, the city's largest park hosts major rallies during Lithuania's drive towards independence, a site to never miss! When it comes to concerts, festivals and exhibitions, the legendary Bernardinai Garden pops up. More into music? Visit the green area next to the White Bridge and enjoy good weather with numerous music and large screen events. If not at Town Hall Square, then where? A hub of trade fairs, celebrations and major events of Vilnius, including the Kaziukas Fair is all housed here. You're lucky if you plan for this vacation in winters. Check out the street art; you will end up smiling with a light heart all the way. If you made it to the Genocide Museum, you get an idea about what Vilnius in past was like. You can also include a visit to Bernardine Cemetery in your list. If you are seeking cuteness in Vilnius, plan a visit to its Jewish Quarter; it is beyond peaceful here! With super cool bars, a beautiful old town, an abundance of history, real epic views and immensely great street art, Vilnius is a trip to remember forever. But hey, that’s not all there is in Lithuania! Here are some of the handpicked weekend break destinations for the Best Day Trips from Vilnius:

1. Kaunas

For a light, cool day trip, pick Kaunas, the second largest of Lithuania. Abundant with cafes, shops and restaurants, Kaunas is a mega destination to explore. Mark local favorites: Green Cafe and Caffe Mio as your must visits! The picturesque brick Kaunas Castle from the 14th century and the medieval old town will fill in your day trip with pictures, snaps and selfies. Other than that, this city lingers on in the flavors of love where brides and grooms can be seen in the City Hall Square. Get the perfect shot at the oldest building in Kaunas, Vytautas Church. This city is as amazing as it gets! The best and most popular way among tourists to get from Vilnius to Kaunas is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 1 hour 10 minutes. Other travelling options include a bus which would take 1 hour and 30 minutes. Alternatively, a taxi can also be hired that takes you to the destination in around 1 hour and 15 minutes.

2. Trakai

When it comes to embracing natural beauty, pick Trakai. One of the Lithuania’s five national parks, Trakai sparkles with elegance and freshness during day time. This day trip from Vilnius has outstanding landscapes with stunning blue lakes that are surrounded by stands of evergreen trees. The highlight here is Trakai Island Castle. Visit this extra touristy spot, explore the unusual collection of items from the 15th and 16th centuries and make your trip selfie worthy! Mark Karaite town with the Karaite church, museum and cute, colorful, wooden homes. Strolling around the town of Trakai is not to be missed, get a taste of Karaite culture! The most preferred mode of transport among people from Vilnius to Trakai is a personal car taking a total travelling time of about half an hour. Other travelling options include a bus which would take nearly 40 minutes. With non availability of bus, a taxi can also be hired which will take you to the destination in nearly the same time as a personal car.

3. Rumsiskess

If you are into Lithuanian culture, Rumsiskes welcomes you. Rumsiskes Open Air Museum of Lithuania is a must visit! One of the largest open air ethnographic museums in Europe, the Open Air Museum comprises of historic homes and numerous more places to click random pictures by. The Zemaitija region has a wooden windmill and friendly goats. Visit homes decorated with traditional furnishings. The Suvalkija region comprises of bee hives, homesteads and is a home of a forest dweller. The Rumsiskes Open Air Museum of Lithuania is by far the best way to end the day trip! From Vilnius to Rumsiskes, the best mode of transport would be a personal car taking a total travelling time of around an hour. Other travelling options include a bus that would take 1 hour and 10 minutes. With non availability of bus, a taxi can also be hired which will take you to the destination in a little less than hour.

4. Ninth Fort

An impressive fort and memorial, Ninth Fort will add a unique flavor to your Vilnius day trip! The museum and memorial opened at the Ninth Fort strive to depict life in exile during Russian occupation of Lithuania in the 1940s. Exciting! Historic! This fort holds immense amount of history and stories about the past self of this region. Dive deep in these stories, fall in love with the aura and end your day trip with knowledge and selfies. The most preferred mode of transport among people from Vilnius to Ninth Fort is a personal car taking a total travelling time of a little over an hour. Other travelling options include a bus which would take 2 hours and 20 minutes. With public transit being so time consuming, a taxi can also be hired if you are time crunched, which will take you to the destination in around 1 hour and 11 minutes.

5. Grutas Park

Adventure + photographs + family time = Grutas Park. An open air sculpture park and museum comprising of old Soviet statues, this destination is an all rounder when it comes to tasting all flavors of an ideal day trip! Get to click pictures with life statues of Joseph Stalin, Vladimir Lenin, and other Soviet figures in the park. The vibes here are all unique and a little eerie. Listen to the Soviet music, tanks and get to know about the indoor expositions out here. Take your kids to the zoo here! The most preferred mode of transport among people from Vilnius to Grutas Park is a personal car taking a total travelling time of around 1 hour 30 minutes. Other travelling options include a bus which would take more or less an hour’s time. Alternatively, a taxi can also be hired which will take you to the destination in around 1 hour and 30 minutes.
